Giang's Favorite Plants!

Hi! Thanks for visiting my page :) I love plants and I thought I'd make a webpage to show you my top picks for house plants!


Having house plants in the home can bring a vibrant and refreshing aesethic to your place! Plants have the ability to also clean the air you breathe so the benefit isn't all just looks. Over the years, houseplants have become more and more popular as they became a favorite past-time hobby for many during the pandemic. Having something to take care of sure gives you a sense of responsibility and rewards you with a healthy looking plant! Below you will find a list of my favorite house plants and how to take care of them.

Plant Name

Care

Looks

Monstera


These are my favorite house plants. Their unique ripped leaves gives them a look like no other. One of my favorite lower classification of the Monstera is the Monstera Delisciosa or the Swiss Cheese Plant. A very fitting name in my most honest opinion. 		They thrive in warm and humid environment which makes them ideal for indoor growth.
  • Water every 1-2 Weeks depending on temperature of room. Hotter room means water more often.
  • 5-8 hours of indirect sunlight a day.

Alocasia Zebrina


Commonly known as the Zebra plant due to its stripped patterns! They are native to the Philipines 		Since the Alocasia Zebrina comes natural habitat is in the rainforest, you'll want to keep them well hydrated.
  • Water them weekly and frequently mist the soil to keep it moist.
  • 5-8 hours of indirect sunlight a day.
  • Keep away from the cold

Calathea Rufibarba


They have some of the softest leaf you'll ever feel! Native to the Americas 		These plants need a different routine based on the season.
  • Water once every week during growing season (Spring-Summer) and once every two weeks during the colder seasons (Fall-Winter).
  • 5-8 hours of indirect sunlight a day.

Hoya Heart


Aren't these just so cute? They are also known as the Lucky Heart. They are native to South-East Asia. 		Hoya Heart strive in dry and hot weather.
  • Water every 2-3 Weeks depending on temperature. Don't worry these plants like it dry.
  • 5-8 hours of direct or indirect sunlight a day

Green Sansevieria


Commonly known as the Snake Plant and is one of the best plant for air filteration. They are native to West Africa. 		These plant were built to survive the West African outdoors. They're tough so care is easy.
  • Water every 1-2 Weeks depending on temperature of room. Hotter room means water more often.
  • 5-8 hours of indirect or direct sunlight a day.
